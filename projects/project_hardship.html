Project - Fireboat "Hardship"

UBC - MECH

March - April, 2016
Vancouver, Canada

Background

Marine safety crew of British Columbia regularly encounters challenges with fire and smoke in coastal waters. In order to respond to fires, particularly in the shallow waters near shore, fast fireboats with high load capacities are needed.

  • Highest speed: 0.728 m/s
  • Weight: 2.1 kg
  • Retardant capacity: 0.8 L

Purpose

The project centres on the design and construction of a multipurpose firefighting shallow water vessel. The objectives are to implement fast travel, accuracy of retardant delivery as well as maneuverability in fireboat design.

Design Contributions

The nozzle was design to reduce turbulence in water flow as well as delivering the retardant to the aim. The angle of the flow is controlled by servo motor and can be altered in 2 directions: up/down and left/right.

The steering system consists of propeller and rudder. RC remote controls servo motor that turns the rudder to certain angle. The rudder is located right behind the propeller which helps the vessel to turn around sharp corners.

The propeller was supported by underwater motor that provides highest latitudinal thrust and allows the boat to accelerate faster. Single propeller was chosen to decrease the fuel consumption of the fireboat.

The hull of the fireboat was made out wood which makes it 100% sustainable. The round shaped hull provides highest speed and makes the boat easy to maneuver. The sharp front edge reduces the drag.
