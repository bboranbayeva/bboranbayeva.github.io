Porject - Medical Aid UAV for Rwanda

UBC - MECH

September - December, 2016
Vancouver, Canada

Background

One of the world problems touches people who live in the rural areas of developing countries receiving little or very poor health care. A significant impediment is the lack of a reliable transport network for getting patient speciments to a suitable lab for analysis. Rwanda has a total of 12,000 km of roads, of which only 8% are paved and connects the larger cities. The remainder is dirt roads which can become impassable during Rwanda's lengthy rainy season due to mud and flooding. The mission the team was to improve the frequency, speed, and accuracy of diagnosis of illnesses in the rural areas by designing a solution that takes to the skies, and can be made by the people, for the people.

  • Plastic Bottle Fuel Tank
  • Fishing Line Actuator
  • Straight Fixed Wing Airfoil
  • Triple Packaging System
  • Hand-Powered Ballon Line
  • 5.7W Thermoelectric Generator
  • Valveless Lockwood Pulsejet

Purpose

The purpose of this project is to design an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) for the transportation of medical test samples from remote areas of Rwanda to centralized testing facilities for analysis. Objectives of the UAV include quick but safe transport of samples, reduced shipping costs, and delivery through generally impassable terrain.

Main Design Contributions

Pulsejet Fuel Tank

  • Designed to be inexpensive, easily made from locally sourced materials
  • Provides the required flow rate of fuel to keep pulsejet running
  • Very lightweight (~ 146.1 g)

A 2L recyclable plastic water bottle acts as the main reservoir for fuel with the stopper at the neck restricting flow. The operation is based on a pressure difference with fuel flowing from the high-pressure tank to the low-pressure pulse jet chamber.

On-board Power Generation

  • Low ongoing operation and maintenance cost
  • Highly sustainable
  • Reasonably cheap
  • Compact (30 x 30 x 12 mm) and lightweight (0.297 kg)

Utilizing waste heat from the pulse jet, the TEG module combined with heatsink can supply 5.7 W of power based on temperatures around 300 ℃ or higher at the hot side. It allows to generate power on board lowering the cost of operation of the UAV.

Navigation

  • Coiled fishing lines were used for actuator system as an affordable and locally produced alternative to servomotors.
  • Made up of a common and cheap plastic avaiable in the market.
  • Mechanism: a coiled fishing line with a nichrome wire inserted within the coils. It actuates due to the temperature increase from the nichrome wire.

Medical Sample Containment

  • Compact and lightweight design
  • Capacity of 6 samples of 10 mL (e.g. citrated whole blood, stool, sputum, or swabs samples)
  • Ensures safe transport of infectious diseases at required sample temperatures
  • Triple packaging system used by United Nations for delivery of hazardous biological samples
  • Leak-proof recyclable plastic container
  • Contains warning sign and safety measures during UAV failure
  • Child-resistant cap design

Pulse Jet

  • Uses the intertia of the air in the outlet pipe (No mechanical valve) Total weight without fuel: 0624 kg
    Fuel ConsumptionL 39 g/min
    Thrust: 7.4N (enough for minimum travel distance of 40 km per flight)
  • Can run on nearly any type of fuel (kerosene, gasoline, diesel, alcohols)
  • Simple design can be easily made locally in Rwanda from common materials at low cost
  • Low operation and maintenance costs

Wings

Wing design is a fixed straight wing design with FX 75 141 airfoil. Provides 5.12 lift to drag ratio and cruising airspeed 18 m/s assuming 2 kg weight of UAV. The design includes high reliability, low costs, and ease of manufacturing.
