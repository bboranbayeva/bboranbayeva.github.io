Project - Regenerator "Whiplash"

UBC - MECH

January - February, 2016
Vancouver, Canada

Purpose and Background

The purpose of the project is to make a vehicle that uses energy from rolling down a ramp to brake and re-accelerate. Regenrative braking is a growing trend in the automotive industry, as it lowers energy consumption. This project demonstrates regenerative braking on smaller scale.

  • Dimensions: 210 mm x 100 mm x 120 mm
  • Weight: 62.6 g
  • Total cost of materials: 85 CAD

Design Contributions

Acceleration Gear System

Deceleration Gear System

Control System consists of Arduino board, servo and encoder

Ratchet Gear that locks the rear wheels when the spring is fully compressed

Low carbon steel chassis and plastic wheels with rubber coating
