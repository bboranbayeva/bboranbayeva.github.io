Project - Medical Aid UAV for Rwanda

September - December, 2016, Vancouver, Canada

One of the world problems touches people who live in the rural areas of developing countries receiving little or very poor health care. A significant impediment is the lack of a reliable transport network for getting patient speciments to a suitable lab for analysis. Rwanda has a total of 12,000 km of roads, of which only 8% are paved and connects the larger cities. The remainder is dirt roads which can become impassable during Rwanda's lengthy rainy season due to mud and flooding. The mission the team was to improve the frequency, speed, and accuracy of diagnosis of illnesses in the rural areas by designing a solution that takes to the skies, and can be made by the people, for the people.

Project - Regenerator "Whiplash"

The purpose of the project is to make a vehicle that uses energy from rolling down a ramp to brake and re-accelerate. Regenrative braking is a growing trend in the automotive industry, as it lowers energy consumption. This project demonstrates regenerative braking on smaller scale.
